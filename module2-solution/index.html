Chicken The chicken is a domesticated bird species that is widely kept as a source of food, both for their meat and eggs. Originating from Southeast Asia, chickens have been domesticated for thousands of years and are now found all over the world. They are one of the most common and economically important livestock species, with millions of chickens raised for food production.

Beef Beef is a type of meat obtained from cattle, such as bulls, cows, and steers. It is one of the most widely consumed meats in the world and is considered a staple food in many cultures. Beef is rich in protein, iron, and other essential nutrients, and is often used as a main ingredient in a variety of dishes such as burgers, steaks, stews, and roasts. The taste, texture, and quality of beef can vary.

Sushi Sushi is a traditional Japanese dish consisting of vinegared rice served with various toppings such as raw fish, seafood, vegetables, and eggs. Sushi is often associated with the raw fish topping, known as sashimi, but there are many different types of sushi, including maki rolls, nigiri, and chirashi bowls. Sushi originated in Japan over 1,000 years ago.
